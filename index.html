About the Companion

The companion supports medicines regulatory assessment by helping users organize review questions, identify evidence gaps, navigate guidance, and prepare draft wording for expert review.

Project Purpose

To demonstrate a responsible approach to digital support in a regulated professional context. The companion brings together public guidance, approved non-confidential assessor themes, and practical review structures.

Current Assessor Guidance and Draft Comments

This section is written as non-confidential public-facing content. It can later be updated with approved internal guidance extracts and sanitized draft comments.

Assessor Guidance

Assessment Framing

Start with the regulatory question, identify the CTD location, summarize the evidence provided, and define the potential quality, safety, or efficacy impact.

Prompt pattern: Identify the issue, the affected dossier section, the missing evidence, and the requested corrective action.
Draft Comments

Clear Deficiency Wording

Comments should be specific, actionable, proportionate, and supported by the submitted information. Avoid vague or unsupported statements.

Example style: Please provide the complete signed and dated specification, analytical procedure, and validation or verification report.
Classification

Screening vs Assessment

Screening confirms whether required documents are present. Assessment evaluates whether the submitted evidence is scientifically acceptable.

Use the companion to route comments to the correct stage and avoid mixing administrative gaps with technical conclusions.
Quality Review

Evidence Alignment

Check consistency between Module 3 documents, QOS or QIS summaries, certificates of analysis, batch data, and proposed commitments.

Prompt pattern: Compare specifications, methods, validation data, COAs, and stability conclusions for alignment.
Escalation

Specialist Input

Flag questions that may need quality, clinical, bioequivalence, GMP, or labelling assessor input before a conclusion is reached.

Examples: uncertain BE applicability, unusual impurity controls, complex formulation risks, or unresolved GMP status.
Review

Human Verification

Outputs are drafts. A qualified assessor must confirm the accuracy, tone, scientific basis, and procedural appropriateness.

Review check: Is the comment accurate, necessary, proportionate, and supported by the evidence?

Disclaimer

A clear boundary statement for responsible use in a regulated professional context.

The Medicines Regulatory Assessment Companion is a portfolio prototype designed to support assessment thinking, learning, guidance navigation, and non-confidential drafting. It does not replace official guidance, expert judgement, internal quality procedures, or formal regulatory decisions.

No official decision-making The companion does not approve, reject, prequalify, or make binding regulatory conclusions.
Human review required All outputs must be checked by a qualified assessor before professional use.
Confidentiality protected Applicant-specific data, unpublished reports, and confidential internal documents should not be entered into public tools.
